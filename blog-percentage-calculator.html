What Exactly is a Percentage?

The word "percent" comes from the Latin per centum, meaning "by the hundred". A percentage is simply a way of expressing a number as a fraction of 100. When you say "20%", you mean "20 out of every 100" or equivalently "0.20" as a decimal or "1/5" as a fraction.

Understanding this core idea makes every percentage formula intuitive. You are always asking: out of every 100 parts, how many are relevant here?

"If a class of 500 students has 378 passing — that is 75.6 students for every 100. In other words, 75.6% passed. The formula simply makes this counting-per-hundred idea precise and fast."

The 3 Essential Percentage Formulas (With Examples)

Virtually every percentage problem you will ever encounter falls into one of three types. Learn these three and you are covered for life — in school, at work and in everyday decisions.

🎯 Formula 1 — Find What X% of a Number Is

When to use: Calculating discounts, GST amounts, commission, tips, interest, tax deductions — any situation where you need a specific portion of a total.

Formula
Result = (Percentage ÷ 100) × Total
Example: 20% of 500 = (20 ÷ 100) × 500 = 0.20 × 500 = 100

Real-life examples using Formula 1:

  • 30% discount on ₹800: (30 ÷ 100) × 800 = ₹240 off → Final price = ₹560
  • 18% GST on ₹1,000: (18 ÷ 100) × 1000 = ₹180 GST → Total = ₹1,180
  • 10% tip on ₹1,500 restaurant bill: (10 ÷ 100) × 1500 = ₹150
  • 5% PF deduction from ₹50,000 salary: (5 ÷ 100) × 50000 = ₹2,500

📈 Formula 2 — Percentage Increase or Decrease

When to use: Calculating salary hikes, price changes, stock movements, population growth, revenue growth — any comparison between an old value and a new value.

Formula
Change% = ((New Value − Old Value) ÷ Old Value) × 100
Increase: ₹50,000 → ₹60,000 = ((60000−50000)÷50000)×100 = +20%
Decrease: ₹1,000 → ₹850 = ((850−1000)÷1000)×100 = −15%
💡 Key Rule

A positive result = percentage increase. A negative result = percentage decrease. The ToolsAI Pro calculator automatically shows green for increase and red for decrease — making it visually instant to interpret.

Real-life examples using Formula 2:

  • Salary raised from ₹45,000 to ₹54,000: ((54000−45000)÷45000)×100 = 20% increase
  • Petrol price fell from ₹110 to ₹99 per litre: ((99−110)÷110)×100 = −10% decrease
  • Stock grew from ₹250 to ₹325: ((325−250)÷250)×100 = +30% growth
  • Exam cut-off dropped from 180 to 165: ((165−180)÷180)×100 = −8.3% decrease

🔍 Formula 3 — Find What Percentage X is of Y

When to use: Converting exam marks to percentages, finding conversion rates, calculating market share, understanding ratios — any situation where you know both numbers and need to express their relationship as a percentage.

Formula
Percentage = (Part ÷ Total) × 100
Example: 45 out of 300 = (45 ÷ 300) × 100 = 15%

📝 How to Calculate Exam Marks as Percentage — Complete Guide

For students, this is the most important percentage calculation of all. Understanding how to convert raw marks into percentage scores is essential for comparing performance, meeting cut-off criteria and understanding your standing in competitive exams.

The formula is Formula 3: Percentage = (Marks Obtained ÷ Total Marks) × 100

🎓 Worked Example: What is 378 out of 500 as a percentage?
Step 1: Identify the values
→ Marks Obtained (Part) = 378
→ Total Marks (Total) = 500
Step 2: Apply the formula
→ Percentage = (378 ÷ 500) × 100
→ Percentage = 0.756 × 100
✅ Answer: 378 out of 500 = 75.6%

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

What is 378 out of 500 as a percentage? +
378 out of 500 = 75.6%.

How do I calculate percentage increase between two numbers? +
Use the formula: Percentage Increase = ((New Value − Old Value) ÷ Old Value) × 100.

Example: If your salary increased from ₹50,000 to ₹60,000: ((60,000 − 50,000) ÷ 50,000) × 100 = (10,000 ÷ 50,000) × 100 = 20% increase.

How to calculate 18% GST on any amount? +
GST Amount = (18 ÷ 100) × Base Price = 0.18 × Base Price.

Examples: 18% GST on ₹500 = ₹90 | On ₹1,000 = ₹180 | On ₹2,500 = ₹450 | On ₹10,000 = ₹1,800.

What is the easiest way to calculate percentage in mind? +
For quick mental calculations, use these shortcuts:

10% = Move decimal one place left (10% of 850 = 85)
5% = Half of 10% (5% of 850 = 42.5)
20% = Double of 10% (20% of 850 = 170)
25% = Divide by 4 (25% of 800 = 200)
50% = Divide by 2 (50% of 850 = 425)
1% = Move decimal two places left (1% of 850 = 8.5)

For any other percentage, combine these building blocks. For example, 18% = 10% + 5% + 3% (which is 3 × 1%).
How do I calculate percentage decrease? +
Percentage Decrease uses the same formula as increase, but the result is negative: ((New − Old) ÷ Old) × 100.

Example: A product's price dropped from ₹1,200 to ₹900: ((900 − 1200) ÷ 1200) × 100 = (−300 ÷ 1200) × 100 = −25%, meaning a 25% decrease.
