❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Is it legal to download YouTube thumbnails?
YouTube thumbnails are publicly accessible images hosted on YouTube's servers. Downloading them for personal use, research or educational purposes generally falls under fair use. However, using someone else's thumbnail commercially or republishing it as your own content without permission can constitute copyright infringement. Always obtain permission for commercial use.
Why is the QHD quality option not available for some videos?
YouTube's maxresdefault (1280×720) thumbnail is only generated for videos that have been uploaded with a custom HD thumbnail. Older videos or those uploaded before YouTube introduced this feature may only have the lower resolution versions (HQ, MQ or default). Our tool automatically detects this and falls back to the next available quality.
Can I download thumbnails of private or unlisted videos?
No. This tool can only access thumbnails of publicly available YouTube videos. Private video thumbnails are restricted on YouTube's servers and cannot be accessed by any third-party tool. Unlisted videos — as long as you have the URL — do work, since unlisted does not mean private.
Does this tool work on mobile phones?
Yes, fully. The tool is completely mobile-responsive and works on Chrome, Safari and Firefox on Android and iOS. Simply copy the YouTube URL from the app, paste it into the tool, select your quality and tap Download. The image will save to your Downloads or Gallery folder.
Is my data safe? Do you store the URLs I enter?
Your data is completely safe. All processing happens locally in your browser using JavaScript. The URLs you enter are never sent to our servers, never logged and never stored. When you close or refresh the page, all entered data is cleared automatically from your browser's memory.
