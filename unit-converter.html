🔁 The History of Measurement — Why Unit Conversion Matters

The need to measure and convert units is as old as human civilisation itself. Ancient Egyptians measured length using the "cubit" — the distance from the elbow to the tip of the middle finger — while the Romans used "mille passuum" (a thousand paces, from which we get the word "mile"). The ancient Indian system used units like "angul" (finger width) and "hasta" (hand span). The problem with all of these early systems was obvious: a cubit varied from person to person, a pace from soldier to soldier. Commerce, construction and science demanded something more consistent.

The French Revolution of the 1790s brought a dramatic solution: the metric system. French scientists proposed a universal, decimal-based system rooted in nature — the metre was defined as one ten-millionth of the distance from the Earth's equator to the North Pole along a meridian. This system, refined over two centuries into today's International System of Units (SI), is now used by virtually every country in the world for science, medicine and most commercial activities.

Yet the imperial system — with its inches, feet, miles, pounds and gallons — persists strongly in everyday use in the United States, and partially in the United Kingdom, India and other countries for certain applications. This coexistence of two major measurement systems is why unit conversion tools are needed daily by engineers, students, travellers, cooks, builders and anyone working across international contexts. ⚖️ Weight & Mass Conversion

Weight conversion is essential for international shipping, cooking, medicine, fitness and science. The distinction between weight (a force caused by gravity) and mass (an inherent property of matter) is important in physics, but in everyday usage the terms are used interchangeably and both refer to how heavy something is.

The kilogram is the SI base unit of mass and is defined with extraordinary precision — since 2019 it has been defined by fixing the Planck constant to exactly 6.62607015 × 10⁻³⁴ joule-seconds, making it one of the most precisely defined units in human history. At the other end of the scale, the metric tonne (1,000 kg) is used for heavy goods, vehicles and cargo, while the milligram is essential in pharmacy and chemistry.

The imperial system uses ounces (oz), pounds (lbs) and stone (st, used in the UK for body weight) — none of which divide evenly into each other in a logical decimal fashion. There are 16 ounces in a pound, 14 pounds in a stone and 2,240 pounds in a long ton. 🌡️ Temperature Conversion — Celsius, Fahrenheit & Kelvin

Temperature conversion is unique among unit conversions because it involves both multiplication and addition — unlike length or weight conversions which only require multiplication by a fixed factor. This is because the three major temperature scales (Celsius, Fahrenheit and Kelvin) have different zero points.

ScaleWater FreezesWater BoilsAbsolute ZeroUsed In
Celsius (°C)0°C100°C-273.15°CMost of the world
Fahrenheit (°F)32°F212°F-459.67°FUSA, some Caribbean nations
Kelvin (K)273.15 K373.15 K0 KScience & physics worldwide

The key formulas: °F = (°C × 9/5) + 32 and °C = (°F − 32) × 5/9. Kelvin is simply Celsius shifted by 273.15 — K = °C + 273.15. Kelvin is used in physics because it starts at absolute zero (the coldest possible temperature, where all molecular motion stops), making calculations involving thermodynamic laws cleaner and more intuitive.

📐 Area & 🧪 Volume Conversion

Area Conversion

Area conversions are critical for real estate, agriculture, construction and land measurement. In India, traditional area units like bigha, gunta, cent and ground are still widely used alongside the modern metric units of square metres and hectares. One hectare equals 10,000 square metres — approximately the size of a standard sports pitch. One acre equals 4,047 square metres and is the dominant land measurement unit in the United States, United Kingdom and much of South Asia for agricultural land.

Volume Conversion

Volume conversion is essential for cooking, chemistry, medicine and fuel measurement. The litre is the most commonly used volume unit worldwide, while the US uses fluid ounces, cups, pints, quarts and gallons for cooking — all of which differ from their UK equivalents (a US gallon is 3.785 litres while a UK gallon is 4.546 litres). In medicine and pharmacy, millilitres (mL) and cubic centimetres (cc, which are numerically equal to mL) are the standard units. Our converter supports all major volume units including the US and UK variants.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions (15+ FAQs)

How many cm in an inch?
There are exactly 2.54 centimetres in one inch. This is the defined, exact conversion factor — not an approximation. So 1 inch = 2.54 cm, 12 inches = 30.48 cm (one foot), and 1 cm = 0.3937 inches.
How do you convert Celsius to Fahrenheit?
The formula is: °F = (°C × 9/5) + 32. For example, 100°C = (100 × 1.8) + 32 = 180 + 32 = 212°F. Common reference points: 0°C = 32°F (freezing), 37°C = 98.6°F (body temperature), 100°C = 212°F (boiling). A quick mental trick: double the Celsius value, subtract 10%, then add 32 — this gives a close approximation.
How many kg in a pound?
One pound (lb) equals exactly 0.45359237 kilograms. For practical purposes, 1 lb ≈ 0.454 kg. Conversely, 1 kg = 2.2046 lbs. Common conversions: 10 lbs = 4.54 kg, 50 lbs = 22.68 kg, 100 lbs = 45.36 kg, 70 kg = 154.3 lbs.
How many miles in a kilometre?
One kilometre equals 0.621371 miles. Conversely, one mile equals 1.60934 kilometres. A useful mental shortcut: multiply kilometres by 0.6 to get an approximate miles figure (accurate to within 3%). For example, 100 km ≈ 62 miles, 50 km ≈ 31 miles.
What is a hectare and how many acres is it?
A hectare (ha) is a metric unit of area equal to 10,000 square metres — equivalent to a square that is 100 metres on each side. One hectare equals 2.47105 acres. Conversely, one acre equals 0.404686 hectares. Hectares are widely used in agriculture, land surveying and environmental measurement internationally.
How many litres in a gallon?
This depends on which gallon. A US liquid gallon equals 3.78541 litres. A UK (imperial) gallon equals 4.54609 litres — approximately 20% larger. This difference matters when reading fuel economy specifications, cooking recipes and imported product volumes. Our converter supports both US and UK gallon variants.
How do you convert square feet to square metres?
Multiply square feet by 0.092903 to get square metres. Conversely, multiply square metres by 10.7639 to get square feet. Common conversions: 100 sq ft = 9.29 sq m, 500 sq ft = 46.45 sq m, 1,000 sq ft = 92.9 sq m. This is essential for real estate listings that switch between measurement systems.
What is the difference between mass and weight?
Mass is the amount of matter in an object and is constant regardless of location. Weight is the force exerted on that mass by gravity and varies depending on gravitational field strength. A 70 kg person has a mass of 70 kg everywhere — on Earth, on the Moon and in space — but their weight on the Moon is about one-sixth their Earth weight because the Moon's gravity is weaker. In everyday use, "weight" typically means mass in everyday contexts.
How many feet in a metre?
One metre equals 3.28084 feet, or approximately 3 feet and 3.37 inches. Conversely, one foot equals 0.3048 metres exactly (this is the defined value). So 5 feet = 1.524 m, 6 feet = 1.8288 m, and 1.75 m (common height) ≈ 5 feet 8.9 inches.
What is Kelvin and when is it used?
Kelvin (K) is the SI base unit of thermodynamic temperature. Unlike Celsius and Fahrenheit, Kelvin starts at absolute zero — the theoretical lowest possible temperature (−273.15°C) where all molecular motion stops. Kelvin is used in physics, chemistry, astronomy and engineering because having zero at absolute zero simplifies thermodynamic equations. Normal room temperature is approximately 293–298 K.
How many millilitres in a tablespoon?
In the metric system, one tablespoon equals 15 millilitres. One teaspoon equals 5 millilitres. One cup equals 240 millilitres (US standard). Note that Australian tablespoons are 20 mL rather than 15 mL — an important difference in baking. Our volume converter supports cups, tablespoons, teaspoons, fluid ounces, litres and millilitres.
How do I convert km/h to mph?
Multiply km/h by 0.621371 to get mph. Conversely, multiply mph by 1.60934 to get km/h. Quick references: 60 km/h = 37.3 mph, 100 km/h = 62.1 mph, 120 km/h = 74.6 mph, 60 mph = 96.6 km/h. Speed limit signs in India use km/h while the US and UK use mph — this conversion is essential for international drivers.
Why do different countries use different measurement systems?
The metric system was designed in revolutionary France in the 1790s and adopted internationally through scientific and industrial standardisation. Most countries officially adopted metric through the 19th and 20th centuries. The United States, despite official adoption of SI units in 1875, has retained imperial units in everyday consumer life due to cultural inertia and the cost of converting infrastructure. The UK uses a mix — metric for most official and scientific purposes, imperial for road distances, beer servings and body height/weight informally.
How many inches is 5 feet 7 inches in total?
5 feet 7 inches = (5 × 12) + 7 = 60 + 7 = 67 inches total. In centimetres: 67 × 2.54 = 170.18 cm. This is a very common height in South Asia for males and is equivalent to approximately 1.70 metres.

How many seconds in a day, week and year?
One day = 86,400 seconds. One week = 604,800 seconds. One year (365 days) = 31,536,000 seconds. A leap year = 31,622,400 seconds. These time conversions are essential in computing (Unix timestamps), physics, astronomy and scientific data logging. Our time converter covers seconds, minutes, hours, days, weeks, months and years.
