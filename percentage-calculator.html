📐 Understanding Percentage Formulas — A Guide for Students

A percentage is simply a number expressed as a fraction of 100. The word "percent" comes from the Latin "per centum" meaning "by the hundred." Understanding the three core percentage formulas will help you solve virtually any percentage problem you encounter — in school, at work, or in daily life.

Formula 1: Finding X% of a Number

This is the most common percentage calculation. If you want to find what 20% of 500 is, the formula is:

Result = (Percentage ÷ 100) × Total

So 20% of 500 = (20 ÷ 100) × 500 = 0.20 × 500 = 100. This formula is used for calculating discounts, tax amounts, tips, commission and any situation where you need a specific portion of a whole.

Formula 2: Percentage Increase and Decrease

This formula calculates the relative change between an original value and a new value:

Change% = ((New Value − Old Value) ÷ Old Value) × 100

If a product's price increases from ₹200 to ₹250, the percentage increase is: ((250 − 200) ÷ 200) × 100 = (50 ÷ 200) × 100 = 25% increase. If the price drops from ₹250 to ₹200, the result is a 20% decrease. Our percentage decrease calculator handles both automatically — a negative result indicates a decrease.

Formula 3: Finding What Percentage X is of Y

Use this when you know two numbers and need to express the relationship as a percentage:

Percentage = (Part ÷ Total) × 100

Example: A student scores 72 marks out of 90. Their percentage is (72 ÷ 90) × 100 = 80%. This formula is essential for calculating exam scores, conversion rates, market share, efficiency ratios and any comparison between a part and a whole.
