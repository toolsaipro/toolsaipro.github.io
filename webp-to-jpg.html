🔄 What is WebP and Why Convert it to JPG?

WebP is a modern image format developed by Google in 2010, designed to provide superior compression for images on the web. A WebP image is typically 25 to 35 percent smaller than a comparable JPG image at the same visual quality. For this reason, many websites — including Google, Facebook and YouTube — use WebP images to make their pages load faster.

However, WebP has a significant compatibility problem: it is not universally supported across all devices, software and platforms. Many older versions of Windows cannot display WebP images in the default Photos app. Microsoft Office applications like Word and PowerPoint cannot insert WebP images directly. Many professional photo editing tools, printing services and document management systems do not accept WebP format.

This is why converting WebP to JPG remains an important and frequently needed task. JPG — also written as JPEG — is the most universally supported image format in the world. Every device, every operating system, every image editing application and every printing service supports JPG. By converting your WebP files to JPG, you ensure maximum compatibility and the ability to use your images anywhere without any format-related issues.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Why can't I open WebP files on my Windows PC?
Older versions of Windows — particularly Windows 7 and Windows 8 — do not have native WebP support. Even some installations of Windows 10 cannot open WebP files in the default Photos app without installing additional codecs. Converting your WebP files to JPG is the easiest solution, as JPG is supported natively on every version of Windows without any additional software.
Is there any quality loss when converting WebP to JPG?
There is a minimal quality reduction when converting to JPG because JPG uses lossy compression. However, our tool converts at high quality (90%+ quality level) which makes the difference virtually invisible to the human eye at normal viewing sizes. If you need absolutely lossless output, choose PNG as your output format instead of JPG.
Can I convert WebP to PNG instead of JPG?
Yes. Our converter gives you the choice between JPG and PNG output formats. Select the PNG option before clicking Convert Now and your WebP file will be converted to a high-quality PNG image. PNG uses lossless compression, meaning there is absolutely no quality reduction compared to the original WebP.
Does the converter work on iPhone and Android?
Yes, completely. The WebP to JPG Converter works on all modern browsers including Chrome, Safari and Firefox on both Android and iOS devices. You can select a WebP image directly from your phone's photo gallery and download the converted JPG file to your device in seconds.
Are my images safe when I use this tool?
Yes, completely safe. All image conversion processing happens locally inside your web browser using JavaScript and the HTML5 Canvas API. Your WebP files are never transmitted to any server, never stored in any database and never accessible to anyone other than you. Once you close the page, all image data is automatically cleared from your browser memory.
Can I convert multiple WebP files at once?
Currently, the converter processes one image at a time. To convert multiple WebP files, simply use the Reset button after each conversion and upload the next file. Each conversion takes only a few seconds, so converting multiple files is still very quick.
Why is my converted JPG larger than the original WebP?
This is completely normal. WebP was specifically designed to produce smaller file sizes than JPG at comparable quality. A WebP file is typically 25 to 35 percent smaller than its JPG equivalent. When you convert WebP to JPG, the resulting JPG file will be larger than the original WebP — but it will be compatible with every device and application that supports standard image formats.
